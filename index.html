The smartphone takeover

The first interesting fact I read was that while many people think they are addicted to their phones – and I include myself in that group – the truth is they have just switched activities and now spend the time they used to waste on other activities using their smartphones. And many app creators are employing various tricks to make that happen.

How many times a day do you check your phone? Where does the need to see what's new come from? Why is it that you cannot control yourself? These are the questions I set out to answer, and after some hours of research, I could find some facts I'll now share.

From loads of notifications to psychological tools, app designers certainly know how to grab our attention. Everything we see is there for a reason, and the main goal is generally to keep us entertained. When we wait for content to load or download, this can sometimes take some time. The design of the progress indicators can help remove the frustration of having to wait. For example, a circular or revolving design can be very visually appealing. It is these little tricks that keep us coming back for more. Maybe simpler but also effective is changing colours. Many apps change their icons periodically. Not because they decided other colours are prettier, but because we will notice the difference and therefore feel curious and be willing to see what's new. More often than not, it's only the icon that changes, but by the time we discover that we're back there, using our phones again.

Will I stop checking my phone every five minutes now that I know about these tricks? I don't think so. But at least I know why I want to look at my phone all the time.

Glossary

addicted (adj) enjoying a particular activity very much and spending as much time as you can doing it

content (n) any material, such as writing, pictures, or music, that appears on a website or other electronic medium

effective (adj) someone or something that is effective works well and produces the result that was intended

icon (n) a small picture on a computer screen that you choose by pressing a button with the mouse in order to open a particular program

load (v) to put information or a program into a computer
